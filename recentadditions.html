A revised (and hopefuly final) version of the paper entitled, "Distance sets of well-distributed planar sets", with Izabella Laba, submitted for publication to the Journal of Discrete and Computational Geometry. Many thanks to the referee for helpful remarks that led to these revisions. In this paper we prove that if the distance set of a sufficiently "thick" discrete set, with respect to a convex set K, is separated, then K must be a polygon. We also prove a quantitative version of this result.

December 18, 2002 A revised version of the paper entitled, "Falconer conjecture for random metrics", with Steve Hofmann, submitted for publication to the Israel Journal of Mathematics. In this paper we prove the following version of the Falconer Conjecture: Let E be a compact planar set of Hausdorff dimension greater than one. Then the distance set corresponding to an image of this set under a rotation and an unisotropic stretching almost surely has positive Lebesgue measure.

January 7, 2003   Revised version of the paper entitled, "Sharp rate of average decay of the Fourier transform of a bounded set", with Luca Brandolini and Steve Hofmann. Many thanks to the referee for helpful remarks that led to these revisions. The two main results have been unified and the proofs shortened. The main result says that if a bounded set in d-dimensional Euclidean space is either convex or has a C^{3/2} boundary, than the spherical L_2 average of the Fourier transform of the characteristic function of this set decays of order $-(d+1)/2$ at infinity, same as the point-wise decay rate of the Fourier transform of the characteristic function of the unit ball.

June 24, 2003       R.I.P. Fuglede Conjecture! A few days ago Terry Tao buried the Fuglede Conjecture by exhibiting a union of cubes in five dimensions which has an orthogonal basis of exponentials but does not tile. Terry clearly demonstrates that orthogonality is much easier to come by in higher dimensions than is tiling. It is still plausible that the one-dimensional conjecture may be true. Also, the conjecture in the convex case remains viable.

Coming in a few days: "Weyl type formula for frames and spectra", with M. Kolountzakis. In this paper we prove good estimates for the error term involved in expressing the number of elements of a spectrum of a bounded domain with respect to an exponential basis in terms of geometric properties of the boundary. In this way we unify the asymptotic formula of Beurling/Landau with the spectral gap estimate obtained a few years ago by Iosevich/Pedersen.

November 22, 2003    "Weyl type formula for frames and spectra", with M. Kolountzakis, has been submitted for publication.

Also, "Combinatorial complexity of convex sequences", with M. Rudnev and V. Ten, has also been submitted for publication. The main result of this paper is that the bound on the number of solutions of thdiophantine equation based on a strictly convex sequence with n elements, with d terms on each side, is O(n^{2d-2+2^{-d+1}}). This result says that in some sense, the O(n^{2d-2}) estimate valid for the sequence of  squares of integers, for example, is almost purely combinatorial, not number theoretic, in essence. We conjecture that O(n^{2d-2}) bound should hold for any convex sequence.

Coming soon: Incidence theory in higher dimensions via Fourier analysis (with I. Laba and M. Rudnev). We obtain sharp incidence theorems for families of surfaces and well-distributed points in higher dimensions using L^2 theory of Fourier integral operators and diophantine approximations. We then apply these results to systems of diophantine equations, distance sets, and related problems.
 

2004: Happy New Year!


Jamuary 8, 2004   A more or less final version of the paper, "Fourier analysis and combinatorial geometry", based on the series of lectures I gave in Padova in June 2002 is finally ready.  The paper gives a  very simple proof of the Szemeredi-Trotter incidence theorem due to Szekely and explains a connection between this problem and several problem in harmonic analysis and additive number theory. Several other problems in geometric combinatorics and their connections with Fourier analysis are also described. The final section of the paper discusses the connection between the Erdos Distance Problem and its continuous variant, the Falconer Distance Problem.

The final version of the paper with Izabella Laba, entitled "K-distance, Falconer conjecture, and discrete analogs" is now available. The paper will appear in the proceedings of the Integers conference in number theory, held last summer at the University of West Georgia.

Coming soon: "Generalized Radon transforms, incidence theorems, and Fourier analysis", with I. Laba and M. Rudnev. In this paper we show, roughly speaking, that non-trivial L^2 bounds for averages over families of manifolds can be translated into non-trivial geometric incidence theorems. When the geometric complexity is sufficiently high we are able to obtain stronger results and those available in combinatorial literature. Some corollaries for distance sets with variable coefficient metrics follow.
