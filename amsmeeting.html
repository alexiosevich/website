              The AMS Committee on Education (COE) would like to
              invite you to attend and participate in its annual
              meeting, which will be held Friday and Saturday,
              October 31 and November 1, 2008 in Washington, DC.
               If you are not able to attend yourself, please
              consider sending a representative in your place.
                                                                
              This year's COE meeting will include discussions and
              presentations on various aspects of mathematics
              education.  Tentative plans include a presentation
              from Math for America, a report on the National Math
              Panel Report and Forum, discussion and examples on
              how to encourage and help students into graduate
              education in mathematics, and reports from the NSF on
              programs and initiatives.
                                                                
             The new NSF Proactive Recruitment in Introductory
              Science and Mathematics (PRISM) program will be part
              of our agenda.  The NSF-DMS website says this about
              the PRISM program:
                                                                
             "The goal of the program in Proactive Recruitment in
              Introductory Science and Mathematics (PRISM) is to
              strengthen the nation's scientific competitiveness by
              increasing the numbers of well-prepared, successful
              U.S. undergraduate majors and minors in science and
              mathematics.  The program will fund innovative,
              potentially transformational partnerships between the
              mathematical sciences and other science or
              engineering disciplines that widen the cross section
              of the mathematical sciences to which freshman and
              sophomore students are exposed and that provide these
              students increased opportunities for research
              experiences involving the mathematical sciences."
               You can view details of the program on the NSF
              website at
              http://www.nsf.gov/publications/pub_summ.jsp?ods_key=nsf08596
              <http://www.nsf.gov/publications/pub_summ.jsp?ods_key=nsf08596>
