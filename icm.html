University of Rochester and the International Congress of Mathematics

The International Congress of Mathematics (ICM) is the premier venue for world class mathematicians from all major areas of mathematics to present their work. The first ICM was held in Chicago in 1983, featuring David Hilbert, Adolph Hurwitz,  Felix Klein and Hermann Minkowski, along with many other luminaries of the late 19th century mathematics. Meetings were held roughly every four years since then, except for a 14 year gap from 1936-1950 due to the Second World War. The Fields Medals, the Nevanlinna Prize, the Gauss Prize, and the Chern Medal are awarded during the opening ceremony. For more detailed information about the International Congress of Mathematics and the complete list of speakers over the years, one can take a look at the very wikipedia entry on this subject matter, or go to the ICM official website.

The University of Rochester currently has two former ICM speakers on faculty: Doug Ravenel and Fred Cohen. Professor Ravenel delivered a lecture, entitled "Complex cobordism and its applications to homotopy theory" at the ICM held in Helsinki in 1978, in the Topology section. Professor Cohen gave a talk, entitled "Applications of loop spaces to classical homotopy theory" at the 1983 ICM in Warsaw, also in Topology.
