Young Mathematicians and Physicists Network

The purpose of this web page is to establish a national network for aspiring young physicists and mathematicians. Over the years I had the privilege of working with several talented high school students who had a keen interested in mathematics, including Tyler Salisbury-Jones, Allen Liu and Davey Fitzpatrick. I met Tyler when he walked into my summer program for high school students at the University of Missouri. Allen Liu was initially a student in Graph Theory class at the University of Rochester and Davey Fitzpatrick was recommended to me by a colleague, Steve Gonek, who was previously approached by Davey's father. These experiences made me wonder how many other young mathematicians out there simply do not have an idea who they might approach about studying mathematicians beyond their school curriculum. It would be great if the students could get on a website, type in their zip code and get a list of people living in the immediate vicinity who could help them realize their potential.

In order to maintain proper standards and take the necessary safety precautions, I will initially only post lists of potential mentors who are current math or science teachers at accredited elementary schools, high schools or universities. In the future, I hope to expand the list to include retired faculty and volunteers from outside the academic world.

In addition to contact information, this page is going to contain links to a variety of mathematical resources such as Math Circles, school mathematics clubs, online learning materials and so on.
