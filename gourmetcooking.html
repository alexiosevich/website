Spicy Seafood Soup:

This recipe resulted from watching several cooking shows over a period of several weeks, confusing and forgetting the resulting information and wild experimentation. Here is the result.

Ingredients: 1 lb. large shrimp (the larger the better...) 1lb. of large sea scallops, 1 lb. of any firm fish fillets cut into bite size pieces, 2 carrots, 3 zucchini, 1 yellow squash, mushrooms, three tomatoes, chopped onions, lots of garlic, hot peppers, cayenne pepper, and white wine.

Directions: Heat up some good olive oil in a large cooking pot. Add some finely chopped onions and pre blenched carrots. A few minutes later add the zucchini, yellow squash, mushrooms, hot peppers and lots of garlic. A minute later add the shrimp, scallops, fish fillets and hot peppers. Immediately add the finely chopped tomatoes. Season with cayenne pepper and salt. Add 1/2 cup of white wine. Let it cook for about 10 minutes. Taste it for the salt content before removing from the pan. Serves 4 hungry people or as many as 6 normal people.

Kielbasa 92:

This is a modification of a recipe I got from Kevin Rowney who got it from Maria Morril. The name of the recipe has evolved over the years... The recipe itself has done some evolving of its own... The list of people who particularly enjoy this dish includes Marco Rigoli and Leonardo Colzani.

Ingredients: Polish sausage, ham, shrimp, regular rice, beef bouillon cubes, red beans, dark beer, lots of garlic and cayenne pepper.

Directions: Brown some Polish sausage and ham in a frying pan with some good olive oil. Add some water, beef bouillon cubes  and rice to the frying pan. Keep adding liquid in the form of dark beer to keep the rice from drying up. About 15 minutes later add lots of garlic and season with cayenne pepper. When rice is about 10 minutes from being done, add the shrimp and red beans. Depending on the size of the frying pan, this will feed anywhere from 2 people to everybody on the planet.

Spicy pasta with tomato sauce:

Yep, your eyes are not deceiving you. Just a simple pasta with tomato sauce. We decided to describe this simple dish on our web page because we still see hordes of silly people buying bad pasta sauce in the supermarket instead of making it themselves. Here is all you have to do. Get a bunch of regular tomatoes and a bunch of cherry tomatoes. Cut the regular tomatoes into small pieces while pretty much leaving the cherry tomatoes alone. Saute the tomatoes in a frying pan with olive oil, garlic and cayenne pepper and salt. We sometimes add a touch of curry when we are bored. Keep cooking at a relatively high temperature until the tomatoes turn into a sauce like substance. Put a cooked pasta (don't overcook it!) into the frying pan and mix it all together.

Try it and I promise you that you will never want to buy a pasta sauce from a jar again.

Beef and oyster stew

Another simple dish. The ingredients are: two pounds of beef stew meat, two cans of oysters, a pound of carrots, three large onions, six medium sized tomatoes,  two heads of garlic, plenty of paprika, salt and pepper, red wine, lemon, and lots of olive oil. Heat up some olive oil in a stewing pot, cut up three large onions into medium sized pieces and get started. After the onions cook down a bit, add pre-blenched carrots cut into small pieces, followed by tomatoes, large chunks of garlic, and the lemon. Cover everything by a generous layer of paprika. After about fifteen minutes, add pre-browned cubes of beef, also covered by a generous layer of paprika, and let the whole thing cook for about ten minutes. At this point add the oysters and cover everything by a cup (or two) of red wine. Let the resulting mixture cook for another fifteen minutes, add salt and pepper to taste and enjoy!
