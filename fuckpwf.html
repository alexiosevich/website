On-line Journal of Analytic Combinatorics


Raison d'etre:

The last decade has seen a flurry of exciting new results that have utilized analytic methods to solve combinatorial problems. These methods include Fourier analysis, functional analysis, analytic number theory, spectral operator theory, and many others. Conversely, there have also been exciting advances in the applications of combinatorics to analysis, most notably to harmonic analysis. There is a growing need for a journal specifically dedicated to publishing this type of work and providing a natural habitat in which it can flourish. For these reasons we decided to launch the Online Journal of Analytic Combinatorics  (OJAC).

We will publish papers on a wide range of subjects, from analysis to number theory to combinatorics, with emphasis on the convergence and interactions between these fields.  We particularly encourage submission of articles which may have one of the following features:

1. Combinatorial results and analytic methods.
2. Analytic results and combinatorial methods.
3. A mixture of combinatorics and analysis in the methods or in their applications.

We expect that this will often include papers which, because of their interdisciplinary nature, may be hard to classify and difficult to publish in a journal specializing in only one area of mathematics.

We also encourage submission of short letters (1-3 pages) describing open research problems of current interest that fall within the scope of the journal.

All contributions will be peer-refereed. Our pledge to the authors and readers is to ensure a timely and competent refereeing process, followed by prompt publication in the case of accepted papers.  Each paper will be refereed by two experts who will be paid $50 each for a timely report.

Our journal will be available electronically, free of charge. As a result, it will be easily accessible to mathematicians around the world who might otherwise be deterred from subscribing due to financial considerations.  At a later date, we may consider publishing a printed version of this journal but only if this can be done without compromising the principles and pledges stated above.

The authors of the accepted papers will receive 50 off-prints free of charge. The off-prints will be produced by the University of Missouri with the artistic input from Sinai Robins.



Information for readers and authors:

This journal will start out as an on-line journal. Its eventual permanent web address is http://www.ojac.org. The temporary web address of the journal is http://www.math.missouri.edu/~iosevich/ojac.html. The first issue is scheduled to come out on January 15, 2006.

As we mention above, we will request two referee reports for each paper. Each referee will be paid $50 if he or she submits the report in four weeks or less.

Papers are to be submitted electronically to ojac@math.missouri.edu in either AMS TeX, LaTeX, or AMS LaTeX formats. Instead of sending us a file with the paper, the authors are welcome to simply send us a link to the arxiv submission.
