Books we have recently enjoyed

On this page we shall put titles and descriptions of books, both mathematical and non mathematical, that one of us has recently
read and really liked. If you have any comments or suggestions, please email us at joshuaiosevich@netscape.net.

Proofs from the book, Martin Aigner and Gunter Ziegler (book-mathematics)

Erdos often talked about The Book where God maintains the perfect proofs of mathematical theorems. In this wonderful book, originally started by Erdos himself, Aigner and Ziegler give beautiful proofs of numerous fundamental results in number theory, combinatorics, analysis, and other areas.

Freedom and its betrayal; six enemies of human freedom, Isaiah Berlin (book-philosophy/political science)

In this wonderful book, Isaiah Berlin, one of the most respected thinkers of the 20th century, takes on some of the icons of Western liberal thought: Helvetius, Rousseau, Fichte, Hegel, Saint-Simon, and Maistre. He attempts to expose their views as dangerously totalitarian in nature, in spite of outward appearances. While exaggerations are almost inevitable in this type of analysis, Berlin's arguments are bold, non-trivial and very original.

A detailed review of this book will appear on this page at some point. 

Fourier Analysis: An Introduction (Princeton Lectures in Analysis, by Elias M. Stein and Rami Shakarchi (book-mathematics).

This is a beautiful introduction to Fourier analysis in many of its manifestations. In addition to fairly standard material, the book discusses applications to number theory, geometry and other areas. The book is highly readable and requires very little background- a perfect text for first year graduate students or advanced undergraduates.
