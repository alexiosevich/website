Sums and Products

THIS PAGE IS UNDER CONSTRUCTION!

The purpose of this page is to keep track of the rapidly developing field of sum-product estimates. The basic problem is the following. Let A be a finite subset of the ring R. Then either the sum-set A+A or the product set A*A is much bigger in size than A. This is not true, of course, in the generality it is stated, since $R$ could be a finite field and A a subfield, but, as we shall see, the principle holds in a wide variety of situations.
