The legend of a young sturgeon




This page is dedicated to the legend of a young sturgeon. On Thursday, February 27, a group of Schrodinger Institute program on Combinatorial and Number theoretic methods in analysis converged upon a strange looking Russian restaurant in Vienna. The service was even stranger, as we soon discovered. Many aspects of that evening could be considered note-worthy, but we shall concentrate on the issue at hand. (A large number of photographs from that night will soon become available on my web page under the Friends and Family heading). Professor Oskolkov ordered a sturgeon, a typical Russian dish. After a long wait, the waiter unceremoniously dumped a plate of whitewish on the table and started walking away. Oskolkov was understandbly perturbed and requested an explanation. After a few minutes of "yes it is", "no it's not", "yes it is", "no it's not", the waiter left to consult with the chef. A minute later, he emerged with the explanation that the reason the fish does not look like a sturgeon, is because it is a "very very young sturgeon"... Those who heard the exchange nearly died laughing. Oskolkov then proposed that from now on, every false theorem be called a "young sturgeon". Thus the legend of a young sturgeon was born!

We ask those with similar legends to send them to me. I will happily add those stories to this page.
