Raise  recommendations

The following set of recommendation refers only to the annual raise pool given to the department. The special merit raise pool, if available, is at the Chair's discretion with advice from the Executive Committee. Among the factors that the Executive Committee may take into account in advising the Chair on this matter are research monographs, quality of invited lectures, inter-disciplinary research, and standing in the mathematical community.

Note: One may get an impression that this document is not consistent with the 40% research, 40% teaching, 20% service principle because (see below) 75% of "merit" money is allocated for research. However, this is not 75% of the total amount but rather 75% of the amount after a certain "average" raise has been allocated.

It has also been noted that four year averages do not appear to come into play here. I envision modifying this scheme after it runs for three years. At the moment, however, it does not seem to make sense to combine this system with the ratings used for the last three years.

Stage 1: Faculty members are divided into four categories.

Category I requirements:

At least one paper in an "A" or "O" journal. (accepted or published in a given year)

A prestigious research award (see Appendix I), an NSF research grant or a comparable foreign research grant, a plenary AMS address, or an invited lecture at the ICM automatically place a faculty member into Category I, provided that he or she has at last one accepted or published refereed paper that year. The aforementioned research grants are counted each year of their duration and for two years afterwards. A plenary AMS address counts for five years. An ICM lecture counts for 10 years. The number of years each award is counted (between 5 and 10) is listed in Appendix I.

A paper in Annals of Math., Inventiones, Journal of the AMS,  or Acta Mathematica automatically place a faculty member into Category I for four years after the paper is accepted.

A paper in Adv. in Math., Duke Math J., or Amer. J. of Math. automatically place a faculty member into Category I for two years after the paper is accepted.

One "O" paper and one "A" paper (or better) also automatically place a faculty member into Category I.

All chaired professors in the department are automatically placed in Category I provided they have at least one refereed paper that year.

An Associate Chair, an Undergraduate Vice-Chair, and a Graduate Vice-Chair are automatically placed in Category II and may be moved up to Category I at Chair's discretion.

Finally, "O" and "A" categories need to be reworked slightly to take Financial Mathematics journals into account.

Category II requirements:

At least one paper  in a "B" journal or higher. (accepted or published in a given year)

Any research grant, including the Missouri Research Board Grant, automatically places a faculty member into category II.

Category III requirements:

At least one refereed paper. (accepted or published in a given year)

Any faculty member not satisfying the requirements of the first three categories is placed into Category IV.

Stage II: Each faculty member in Category I, II and III shall receive an equal percentage raise. This percentage shall be determined by the Chair and will (of course) be less than or equal to the total percentage raise allocated to the department by the university.  Each faculty member in Category IV will also receive this basic raise provided they satisfy at least one of the following requirements:

(1) At least one refereed published or accepted paper (in mathematics related disciplines) the previous year.

(2) Teaching evaluations in the top 20% when compared to the ratings others have received in the same class.

(3) Served as a course coordinator.

The remaining individuals receive a lower percentage raise to be determined by the Chair with advice from the Executive Committee.

Stage III: The remaining funds (to be assigned as lump sums, not percentage raises) are divided into two categories: research (75%) and teaching/service (25%).

The research and teaching/service money is then distributed among all eligible faculty members according to the criteria outlined below.  

Stage IV: 2/3 of the research money is distributed equally among faculty members in Category I. The remaining 1/3 of the research money is distributed equally among faculty members in Category II. The teaching/service money is distributed equally among faculty members satisfying at least one of the following criteria:

(1) Teaching evaluations in the top 10% when compared to the ratings others have received in the same class. This applies to undergraduate classes only.

(2) Significant extra-curricular activities related to teaching or service. This determination is to be made by the Executive Committee but a thorough explanation must be given in each individual case.

(3) Advised a Masters or a Ph.D. student.

(4) NSF Grant in Mathematics Education.

(5) At least one refereed publication in Mathematics Education.

(6) Authorship of a textbook.


Special merit raise recommendations

The following criteria should be used:

(1) Member of the Executive Committee are not eligible for a special merit raise for the duration of their term.

(2) The main criterion for a special merit raise should be a specific research accomplishment viewed as outstanding by the elite members of the mathematical field in question. Concrete and verifiable evidence must exist that this accomplishment has enhanced the reputation of the department.

(3) The Associate Chair, the Director of Graduate Studies and the Director of Undergraduate Studies may receive a special merit raise as compensation for dimished opportunity to engage in teaching and research.
