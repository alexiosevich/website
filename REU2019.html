Summer REU 2019: Explorations in Data Science

Data Science has taken both the academic and industrial scene by storm in the past decade. While the general concept-the analysis of large data sets- is at the core of this discipline, it would be difficult to pinpoint what precisely data science is and what it is not. In this summer program I would like to explore what elementary mathematical techniques have to say about the analysis of large data sets. The emphasis will not necessarily be on the techniques that are currently prevalent in the data science industry. My thought is that if you learn how to improvise and introduce new ideas, you will do well as you can learn or have already learned the standard fare in your undergraduate computer science or data science classes.

We are going to meet twice a week at an agreed upon time. We shall begin with a crash course on python. My guess is that most of the participants will arrive knowing more python than I do. This would be great because I hope to learn in this program as much as I look forward to teaching. We shall follow this up with a crash course on basic statistics. As the next step, we shall brainstorm some classical and accessible mathematical concepts that have a bearing on the analysis of large data sets. The emphasis will be new angles and ideas which we shall test by writing python code and applying it to a variety of data sets that we shall obtain from open sources.
