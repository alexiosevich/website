The topics we will be research during the Fall 2024 semester are yet to be determined, but with very high probability we are going to run a research group on signal recovery. The basic idea is the following. Suppose that f:ℤNd→ℂf: {\mathbb Z}_N^d \to {\mathbb C} and we wish to transmit it via its Fourier transform f(x)=∑m∊ℤNdχ(x⋅m)f^(m).f(x)=\sum_{m \in {\mathbb Z}_N^d} \chi(x \cdot m) \widehat{f}(m).However, if some of the frequencies are missing, for example if {f^(m)}m∊S{\{\widehat{f}(m)\}}_{m \in S} are unobserved for some S⊂ℤNd.  The question we ask is, under what reasonable assumptions can we still recover the function (or the signal, as electrical engineers tend to call it) exactly? We are also going to explore some applications of signal recovery to data science. This project ran during the StemForAll2024 workshop this summer and is going to continue into the Fall. Next summer, the entire StemForAll2025 workshop will be dedicated to the pure and applied aspects of signal recovery.

Another project that is likely to run during the Fall semester is the investigation of the Erdos distance problem on Riemannian manifolds. The basic question is the following Let EE be a finite subset of MM, a d-dimensional compact Riemannian manifold without a boundary. Let Δ(E)\Delta(E) denote the distance set of EE with respect to the Riemannian metric, i.e Δ(E)={ρ(x,y):x,y∊E}\Delta(E)=\{\rho(x,y): x,y \in E\}, where ρ\rho is the Riemannian metric on MM. In Euclidean space it is conjectured that |Δ(E)|≥c|E|2d |\Delta(E)| \ge c{|E|}^{\frac{2}{d}}, up to logarithmic terms, with the example provided by the n1d×n1d×…×n1dn^{\frac{1}{d}} \times n^{\frac{1}{d}} \times \dots \times n^{\frac{1}{d}} piece of the integer lattice. On general manifolds, such rich arithmetic does not typically exist, which makes it unclear what the manifold variant of the Erdos distance conjecture should even say. This is a fascinating topic with lots to explore!
