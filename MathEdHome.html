Views on MathEd and its impact on serious research and education



Dedication: This page is dedicated to all the undergraduate students I have ever taught. The educational system under the tutelage of MathEd did not manage to break their spirits or destroy their talents, but it made their lives more difficult than was necessary. It is up to us to make sure that no further damage is done to the current and future students. MathEd does not only soak up funds that are badly needed elsewhere- it actually contributes to the continued deterioration of the mathematics education in this country.

Raison d'etre: The purpose of this web page is two-fold:

-- To increase public awareness of MathEd and the damage that I believe it is doing to the mathematics education in this country. This page will contain essays and articles reflecting my views on MathEd and other opinions on this subject that I find interesting. Your help is urgently needed! If you know a good MathEd horror story, please send it to me at iosevich@math.missouri.edu. I may ask for some references and supporting materials, but I will then most likely include your story on this page.

-- To keep track of non-MathEd educational activities around the country performed by serious mathematicians. If you are involved in something like that and you are a pure (or applied) mathematician, please email me at iosevich@math.missouri.edu.

Educational activities by serious mathematicians

For the time being, I will just list my own activities, but that will change soon as I start receiving information from other mathematicians.

-- Summer program on the Erdos distance problem: This program will be run by Julia Garibaldi, Alex Iosevich, and Tyler Jones-Salisbury at the University of Missouri-Columbia, August 1-5 in MSB 110, 1:00-4:00 p.m.. The purpose of the program is to familiarize motivated high school students with the Erdos distance problem which asks for the minimal number of distances determined by a finite point set in the Euclidean space. Julia Garibaldi is a postdoctoral researcher at Georgia Tech. Tyler Jones-Salisbury is a high school students who participated in a similar program I ran last summer.
